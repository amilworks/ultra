# Smithsonian Prairie Conservation Language

Use this reference when the turn explicitly mentions Smithsonian collaborators, NZCBI, black-footed ferrets, or project-specific conservation framing.

- Prefer survey language such as "detected prairie dogs", "visible burrow entrances", and "monitoring observations" over absolute census language.
- When relevant, note that prairie dogs are keystone species and that colony structure can matter for associated conservation targets such as black-footed ferrets.
- Keep management claims conservative: detections can support monitoring and habitat assessment, but they do not by themselves establish trend, occupancy, or habitat quality without repeated surveys and site context.
- If the imagery covers only a tile or local subset of a colony, make the local scope explicit in the answer.
